# Logistical Blueprint: Natural Organic Packaging and Hospital Transit
## Standard Operating Procedures for Distribution and Cold-Chain Operations

### 1. Organic Structural Packaging
To preserve compound integrity without relying on synthetic polymers, all processed lots are housed within multi-layer, plant-based lipid matrices:
*   **Inner Containment Sleeve:** Constructed from a structural, high-density blue corn lipid matrix layer processed via lime fruit treatment to create a durable capsule.
*   **Outer Protective Layer:** Coated with a purified, hydrated beeswax shell layer. This creates a natural barrier against moisture entry and prevents atmospheric gas transfer.
*   **Food-Grade Certification:** All packaging materials must be strictly certified food-grade, ensuring absolute safety for medical-logistical transport.

### 2. Cold-Chain Logistical Parameters
The biological and physical performance of finished batches requires strict thermal boundaries during transit.

*   **Temperature Restrictions:** Materials must be maintained continuously between **2.0°C and 6.0°C**. Any single excursion crossing outside these boundaries must immediately trigger a compliance deviation ticket.
*   **Sensor Hardware Deployment:** Every shipping unit is equipped with a KiCad-verified telemetry module reading data directly from a PT100 probe. Telehealth logistics tracking software monitors these parameters continuously from the Yesler Towers command center.

### 3. Critical Logistical Paperwork Requirements
Every outbound batch shipment must contain a physical compliance packet enclosed within an exterior waterproof sleeve. This packet includes:
1.  **The Master Bill of Lading (BOL):** Details origin coordinates, carrier IDs, and destination hospital information.
2.  **The Manufacturing Certificate of Analysis (CoA):** Validates spectroscopic purity percentages, lot numbers, and operator initials.
3.  **The FDA 3926 Companion Sheet:** Authenticates the specific Individual Patient Expanded Access IND approval authorizing hospital receipt.
4.  **Chain-of-Custody Handoff Sheet:** Demands a physical signature, time-stamp, and badge id entry from the receiving hospital pharmacist before the transport containers can be legally opened.
