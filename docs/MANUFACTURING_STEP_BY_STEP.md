# Standard Operating Procedure: Molecular Conditioning and Ion Substitution
## Manufacturing Instruction Manual for Processing Operations

### 1. Raw Extraction & Matrix Stabilization
1.  **Milling:** Mechanically grind raw crop materials within jacketed stainless-steel vessels under cleanroom air conditions to yield the raw chlorin matrix.
2.  **Thermal Stabilization:** Chill fluid matrices immediately to **4.0°C (±0.5°C)** to protect the long conjugated polyene chains from temperature-induced oxidation.

#### Step A: Calculating Reduced Mass (\(\mu\))
The natural resonant vibration frequency of any chemical bond depends fundamentally on the masses of the two bound atoms. For a Magnesium-Nitrogen (\(Mg\text{-}N\)) coordinate covalent bond, the reduced mass is formulated as:

$$\mu_{\text{Mg-N}} = \frac{m_{\text{Mg}} \cdot m_{\text{N}}}{m_{\text{Mg}} + m_{\text{N}}}$$

*   Atomic mass of Magnesium (\(m_{\text{Mg}}\)) \(\approx 24.305 \text{ amu}\)
*   Atomic mass of Nitrogen (\(m_{\text{N}}\)) \(\approx 14.007 \text{ amu}\)

$$\mu_{\text{Mg-N}} = \frac{24.305 \cdot 14.007}{24.305 + 14.007} = \frac{340.440}{38.312} \approx \mathbf{8.886 \text{ amu}}$$

#### Step B: Calculating the Required Energy Input
To alter or loosen the central coordinate covalent bonds, energy inputs are monitored against the Planck-Einstein relation relative to the bond's activation barrier:

$$E = h\nu$$

Where:
*   \(h\) = Planck’s constant (\(6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J}\cdot\text{s}\))
*   \(\nu\) = Frequency of the energy input wave (Hz)
